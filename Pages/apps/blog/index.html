Earned Income Tax Credit Increases Your Tax Refund

Posted by [email protected] on January 5, 2016 at 10:00 PM comments (0)

Earned Income Credit, commonly referred to as EITC or EIC, and increases your income tax refund. If you file as a single taxpayer or the head of a household with one or more dependents and earn a little too moderate amount of gross income in a tax year, you are eligible for this tax. If you qualify for this income tax credit and are entitled to receive it, it can make your life (and wallet) better as it has for many people over the years. The Earned Income Tax Credit is a financial benefit or relief for employees who earn $ 48,362 or less per year.


There are some things you should know about the EITC:


It is important to constantly keep up with and assess the Earned Income Tax Credit qualification rules to determine whether you qualify due to the fact that your monetary, marital conditions or parental might change with each tax year. If you do not qualify for the previous year, it does not mean you're not in the next, so always check.


If you qualify for the Earned Income Tax Credit, your credit can reach $ 5,666. Not only does the EITC reduce your federal tax, but also can result in a refund. The amount of the EITC you will receive depends on your income and if there are qualifying children in your home.


Once you qualify for EITC, you will be required to put in for an income tax return and actively claim the refund credit.


However, note that if your filing status is Married Filing Separately, you are not eligible for EITC.


You must also have earned income. You have earned income if you are employed by someone or an organization that pays wages, gets income from agriculture, or if you are self-employed. In some cases, you could be collecting disability income.


Single people without children or dependents and married couples may qualify for the EITC. If they have no kids, you must meet the requirements for residency and age and the dependency rules in order to be eligible.


There are special rules for people who are members of the US Armed Forces in combat zones. These individuals can decide to use their combat wages without tax deduction in earned income for the EITC. If this decision is taken, their combat wages will be nontaxable.


To determine whether you are eligible, it is very easy to do that. There is an interactive tool on the website, The EITC Assistant, which gets rid of the guesswork from eligibility rules. You just need to answer a few questions to determine if you are eligible and the approximate amount of your Earned Income Tax Credit.


These are just a few important things you should know about the EITC. You can get more help at IRS Taxpayer Assistance Centers to help you prepare and claim your EITC.
